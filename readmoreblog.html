National Videography-Editing Day

25th January, 2022 · Videography-Editing blog

The national Videography-Editing day is celebrated on 25th of january each year. he purpose of National Videography Day is to recognize those who work as filmmakers and videographers. Like no other kind of communication, video has a way of inspiring and attracting its audience. Every day, video enables us to interact, connect, and communicate with one another.

Why this day got established?
With more than 6 billion films watched each day, video has emerged as one of the most potent and useful technologies available today. People are motivated, engaged, and inspired by it. The goal of the Recognition of National Videography Day is to inspire people to record and share films on January 25 in order to further global connectivity. It would also serve as a day to honor the photographers who frequently risk their lives in order to document news events, conflicts, natural catastrophes, and entertainment. National Camcorder-Day

20 January, 2022 · Camcorder Day History

Camcorders have been there at all of them, including weddings, birthdays, graduations, children's recitals, and even those humiliating moments you don't want anybody to know about. At one point, camcorders were so widely used that a TV program about hilarious home films was created as a result of their popularity. Camcorder Day honors all those priceless moments, videographers, and touching clips.
Ironically, Camcorder Day's beginnings were impossible to document. This day's originator(s) and/or organization are yet unknown. Although the intention behind it—to celebrate the camcorder and all that is important—can be readily understood. The first video cameras had a separate recording unit and were big, hefty devices. The camcorder was a gadget that could record and capture video by combining a video camera with a videocassette recorder. The original camcorders were tape-based and used videocassette cassettes to store analog signals. The tape was supplanted by storage medium as time went on and digital recording became more prevalent (internal flash memory and SD cards).

For more than 50 years, Steve McCurry has been one of the most recognizable names in modern photography. McCurry, who was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, attended Pennsylvania State University to study cinematography before beginning a career in journalism. After two years, McCurry went to India for the first of many visits. He crossed the subcontinent with just a suitcase of clothing and some film, photographing every place he came across. One of the first to convey to the world the savagery of the Russian invasion was McCurry's photography. Since then, McCurry has produced iconic photos on all seven continents and a number of nations. His corpus of work includes depictions of war, extinct societies, old traditions, and modern society, all while maintaining the human aspect that gave rise to the Afghan Girl, a famous image that has become so iconic. Some of the most prominent honors in the field, like as the Robert Capa Gold Medal, have been given to McCurry.
